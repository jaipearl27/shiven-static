Fixed Investment

What are Fixed Investments?

Fixed investments, such as Fixed Deposits (FDs), Recurring Deposits (RDs), and other government (insured) savings products (Public Provident Funds (PPF) & Postal Schemes), are defined as low-risk, safe investments that guarantee payoffs over a defined period. Fixed or guaranteed savings products are suitable for those investors who wish to guarantee capital, a predictable income, and certainty of funds.

  • Fixed deposit: A Fixed Deposit is simply a single lump sum investment for a set term with a set interest rate. After setting your term, funds can also be based on a monthly, quarterly, annual, or maturity basis.
  • Recurring Deposit: A Recurring Deposit is when the investor deposits a specific amount each month, for a defined tenure, less a fee – this product also assists clients with creating a routine of steady savings while earning compound interest.
  • PPF & Postal Schemes: Both Public Provident Fund (PPF) and post office (“savings”) schemes – i.e., investments backed (insured) by the government and provide clients with a suitable rate of return, in addition to tax-cost benefits and guaranteed fund security for the long-term.

These products would generally appeal to the conservative investor as they are guaranteed by the State Sovereign.

Who Offers FD and RD Services?

  • Public Sector Banks: Government sector banks like SBI, PNB, and Bank of Baroda provide FD and RD schemes with high interest rates, long security tenure, and wide-ranging branch networks.
  • Private Sector Banks: HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, and Axis Bank offer FD/RD options with newer digital interfaces. The process is faster and offers higher flexibility in payment.
  • NBFCs (Non-Banking Financial Companies): Bajaj Finance, Mahindra Finance, Shriram Finance, and others are examples of NBFCs that provide corporate FDs that are easy to obtain with no lengthy processes, better interest rates, shorter tenures, and good renewal terms; however, they incur slightly higher risk than banks.
  • Jewellers (Gold Saving Schemes): Prominent jewellers like Tanishq, Malabar Gold, and Kalyan Jewellers offer RD-type monthly gold saving schemes. They are similar to savings schemes, enabling customers to deposit an agreed amount every month, redeemable against gold jewellery purchase at the end of the period. Though not RBI-regulated, these cater to a niche group, saving for jewellery purchase in the future.

These products would generally appeal to the conservative investor as they are guaranteed by the State Sovereign.
