What Is Fire Insurance?

Fire insurance is a policy that is specialized to give financial compensation for loss or damage arising from fire and allied perils. Fire insurance covers buildings, stock, machinery, furniture, and other tangible assets, enabling companies, residents, and institutions to recover from untoward fire incidents. Whether the loss arises due to accidental fire, lightning, explosion, or riot, fire insurance serves as an important shield. For insurance consultants and advisors, providing fire insurance provides high-value opportunities in commercial and residential segments. With Shiven CRM, the sales of fire insurance, renewals, claims, and client interaction become effortless and completely digitised.

Types of Fire Insurance Policies

Shiven CRM enables advisors to ensure that the appropriate policy matches client requirements, depending upon risk profile, asset type, and coverage extent.

Standard Fire & Special Perils Policy

Covers against fire, lightning, explosion, storm, flood, riot, strike, and vandalism. For homeowners, shopkeepers, and small business firms.

Valued Policy

The amount of insured property is fixed at policy start, paying a fixed sum in the event of total loss.

Floating Policy

Provides cover for movable goods at various points under one policy. For traders and distributors.

Specific Policy

Covers a particular asset to a certain amount. Suitable for individual items or premises. .

Comprehensive Fire Insurance Policy

An all-risk policy that goes beyond fire to cover burglary, natural disasters, and accidental loss. Popular among commercial customers and manufacturers.

Key Policy Features

    Instructing clients on fundamental policy terms increases trust and creates reasonable expectations. The following are significant factors your staff needs to inform clients about:
      1. Waiting Periods: The majority of Fire insurance policies come with a waiting period before the ability to claim specific benefits. These include maternity, particular illnesses, or pre-existing conditions.
      2. Portability: Customers may change to another insurer without losing the continuity advantages, including the waiting period credit. Shiven CRM can handle portability requests and paperwork
      3. Pre-Existing Diseases (PED): These are conditions that the client has prior to taking the policy. PEDs are covered after a waiting period specified in the policy.
      4. Exclusions: Exclusions are conditions or treatments that are not covered under a policy. Some common exclusions are cosmetic surgery, self-inflicted damage, and substance abuse treatment.
