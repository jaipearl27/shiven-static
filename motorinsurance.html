What Is Motor Insurance?

Motor insurance is a service designed to financially safeguard vehicle owners against unexpected incidents involving their cars, bikes, or commercial vehicles. It provides coverage for damages due to road mishaps, natural events, or accidental situations and also fulfils mandatory legal requirements. With Shiven CRM, advisors can guide clients through the right motor insurance choices and manage everything from quotations to renewals in one easy-to-use interface.

Main Types of Motor Insurance

Just like every vehicle has a different purpose, insurance too comes with different types tailored to specific needs.

1. Third-Party Insurance 

This policy covers the cost of damages caused by your client's vehicle to another person’s property or vehicle. It is required by law for all motor vehicle owners and is the most basic form of coverage.

  • Ideal for meeting legal obligations
  • Covers third-party property or vehicle damage
  • Simple and cost-effective

    • 2. Comprehensive Insurance

    Comprehensive plans offer wider protection. Along with third-party liability, it includes:

  • Comprehensive plans offer wider protection. Along with third-party liability, it includes:
  • Natural events like floods, storms, or fires
  • Man-made events such as vandalism or external impact
  • Protection against theft or attempted theft

    • 3. Own Damage Cover

    This standalone policy protects the client’s vehicle and can be purchased alongside an existing third-party policy.

  • Suitable for those with newer or higher-value vehicles
  • Offers more control over coverage features

    • Add-Ons: Customize Client Coverage

    Shiven CRM enables your team to seamlessly offer and explain add-ons that enhance the base motor insurance policy. Add-ons provide additional protection and boost client confidence.

  • Popular add-ons include:
  • Zero Depreciation: Helps clients claim the full value without deduction for part wear and tear.
  • Roadside Assistance: Quick support in case of breakdowns or emergencies.
  • Return to Invoice: Reimburses the full invoice value in case of total vehicle damage or irretrievable loss.
  • Engine Protection: Covers non-collision engine-related issues such as oil leaks or water damage.
  • No Claim Bonus Protection: Maintains the client’s bonus even after a claim.
