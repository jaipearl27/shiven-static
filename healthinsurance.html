What Is Health Insurance?

Health insurance is a financial product that helps individuals and families cover the rising costs of medical care. It typically pays for hospitalisation, treatments, diagnostic tests, surgeries, and preventive healthcare. As healthcare costs escalate annually, health insurance offers a crucial safety net, ensuring that people have access to quality care without facing financial ruin. From individuals to senior citizens to entire families, a reliable health insurance plan provides peace of mind and financial security. Key Types of Health Insurance Plans

Each client has different healthcare requirements. Shiven CRM enables your team to provide and administer the appropriate policy to each one.

1. Individual Health Insurance

Protects a single person, including medical bills due to illness, hospitalisation, or accidents. Best suited for young professionals and individuals looking for individual protection

2. Family Floater Plans

A single policy that insures all family members, pooling a collective sum insured. Best for couples, parents, and children under a single premium

3. Senior Citizen Plans

Tailor-made coverage for people aged 60 and older, with benefits appropriate for age-related health requirements. Comprises increased coverage for intensive care, diagnostics, and a longer duration of recovery

4. Critical Illness Insurance

Pays a guaranteed lump sum amount on diagnosis of certain serious diseases such as cancer, stroke, or kidney disease. Intended to alleviate the financial burden of lengthy treatment and recuperation .

5. Top-Up & Super Top-Up Plans

Extra layers of protection that come into effect after a specified level, raising the overall amount of insurance without exorbitant premiums. Beneficial to clients with existing employer insurance who desire extra protection

Key Policy Features

    Instructing clients on fundamental policy terms increases trust and creates reasonable expectations. The following are significant factors your staff needs to inform clients about:
      1. Waiting Periods: The majority of health insurance policies come with a waiting period before the ability to claim specific benefits. These include maternity, particular illnesses, or pre-existing conditions.
      2. Portability: Customers may change to another insurer without losing the continuity advantages, including the waiting period credit. Shiven CRM can handle portability requests and paperwork
      3. Pre-Existing Diseases (PED): These are conditions that the client has prior to taking the policy. PEDs are covered after a waiting period specified in the policy.
      4. Exclusions: Exclusions are conditions or treatments that are not covered under a policy. Some common exclusions are cosmetic surgery, self-inflicted damage, and substance abuse treatment.
